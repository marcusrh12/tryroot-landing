Community Guidelines

Root is a place for genuine debate and honest reasoning. These guidelines exist to keep conversations worth having.

What Root is for

Root questions are meant to be genuinely debatable — issues where reasonable people can disagree based on different values, priorities, or interpretations of evidence.

Good Root questions invite reflection. They don't have obvious answers, they aren't rhetorical, and they aren't designed to provoke rather than discuss.

Submitting a community question

When you submit a question, it goes through an automated review before appearing in the feed. To be approved, your question must:

✅
Be phrased as a genuine question Agree/disagree format — not a statement, rant, or loaded prompt.
⚖️
Be genuinely debatable There should be a reasonable case on both sides.
🌐
Be relevant to a broad audience Not limited to a specific local, regional, or niche context without wider relevance.
🇺🇸
Be in English Root currently serves an English-speaking audience.
🚫
Not target private individuals by name Public figures in their public roles are fair game. Private individuals are not.
🔁
Not be a duplicate Check if a similar question already exists before submitting.
What's not allowed

The following will result in immediate rejection of a submitted question and may result in account action:

Hate speech — including slurs targeting race, ethnicity, religion, gender identity, sexual orientation, or disability status
Content designed to dehumanize any group of people
Spam, promotional content, or questions submitted repeatedly after rejection
Questions deliberately based on false premises presented as fact
Personal attacks on named private individuals
Reporting content

If you see a community question that violates these guidelines, use the report button on the card. Questions that receive 3 or more reports within 24 hours are automatically hidden and sent for review.

Abuse of the report system to suppress legitimate debate is itself a violation of these guidelines.

General conduct

When sharing your reasoning on any question — community or otherwise — keep it focused on the issue, not the person. Responses that attack other users rather than engaging with the question may be removed.

Root does not moderate general profanity in reasoning responses. We trust users to engage like adults.

Questions about these guidelines or want to appeal a rejected question? Reach us at tryroot.app@gmail.com
